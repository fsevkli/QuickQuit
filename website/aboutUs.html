About Us

We are a group of students from Miami Univerity in Oxford, Ohio, U.S.A. We are passionate about technology and want to utilize it to make a positive impact on the world. Our goal for this project is to provide safety to the vulnerable people who need it most.

Our Story

In January 2025 we went on a study abroad to Manchester, England. Here we had the opportunity to learn more about the homelessness crisis facing Manchester while also looking over the websites, Justlife and Lifeshare, which provide resources to people in need. An aspect of these websites that stood out to us was the "get me out" button. We had never seen anything like this before and after asking questions we learned its purpose is to help victims of domestic abuse. Many people in this situation use websites like these to seek resources, however, the abuser walking in on them accessing these sites could put them in more danger. The "get me out" button is used to allow the victim to quickly leave the website before the abuser gets the chance to see what they were on. Unfortunately, we quickly realized none of these buttons worked or if they did work they opened to a blank Google search page, which could be seen as suspicious. In addition, the abuser would still be able to view the search history and see if they were accessing sites for help to escape domestic abuse. From this exploration, we came up with QuickQuit to fix these issues and potentially help save a life.
